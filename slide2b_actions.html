What Each Audience Can Do Right Now

Three crash types. Three agencies. Specific actions tied directly to where the data says the problem is worst.

🏛
Board of Supervisors
Policy, funding, and agency direction
Unsafe Speed
Direct Caltrans to expand AB 645 speed camera enforcement on I-10, I-5, and SR-60 — the corridors your hotspot analysis flagged as the highest-risk in the county.
Unsafe Turns
Direct LADOT to fast-track protected left-turn retrofits at Southeast LA's highest-crash intersections. This is both an infrastructure failure and an environmental justice concern.
Lane Violations
24,005 injuries across all three types = hospital costs, lost wages, and county liability. Even a 10% reduction saves tens of millions annually — the economic case for acting now.
🚦
LADOT
Intersection design and signal programs
Unsafe Turns
Unsafe turns kill at a higher rate per crash than speeding — 2.31 vs. 2.17 deaths per 1,000. Protected left-turn retrofits at Southeast LA intersections are the highest-impact intervention per dollar you can make.
Lane Violations
Improve signage, lane markings, and lighting at complex urban intersections where weaving and lane-change crashes cluster. Small infrastructure changes reduce the conditions that cause these crashes.
Unsafe Speed
Coordinate with Caltrans on speed camera placement at the freeway on-ramps and arterial intersections where high-speed crashes transition from freeway to surface street.
🛣
Caltrans District 7
Freeway infrastructure and safety
Unsafe Speed
Deploy AB 645 automated speed cameras on I-10, I-5, and SR-60 — where 54 people were killed and 14,577 injured in speed-related crashes in 2025 alone.
Lane Violations
Commission an immediate safety audit of the I-110 and US-101 interchange zones where merge conflicts generate a disproportionate share of lane-change crashes. Restriping and lighting upgrades are the fix.
Unsafe Turns
Audit freeway on/off ramp geometry in Southeast LA where turning crashes from surface streets spill onto freeway approaches — a coordination point between Caltrans and LADOT jurisdiction.
