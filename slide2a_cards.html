Not All Crashes Are Equal

Unsafe turns have a higher fatality rate per crash than speeding — 2.31 vs. 2.17 deaths per 1,000 crashes. 24,005 people were injured across all three types in 2025.

Where it happens
Freeway corridors — I-10, I-5, SR-60 — during commute and late-night hours at high speed on roads people rely on daily.
The fix
Expand AB 645 automated speed camera enforcement on I-10, I-5, and SR-60 where hotspot analysis shows the highest concentration of speed crashes.

Where it happens
Dense urban intersections in Southeast LA — quick decisions, heavy traffic, complex junctions. Fewer crashes than speed but a higher kill rate per incident.
The fix
Fast-track protected left-turn signal retrofits at the highest-crash intersections in Southeast LA — an infrastructure failure and an environmental justice concern.

Where it happens
Freeway weaving and merging zones — I-110 and US-101 interchange areas generate a disproportionate share of these crashes.
The fix
Commission an immediate safety audit of I-110 and US-101 interchange zones. Restriping, upgraded signage, and better lighting reduce lateral conflict at merge points.
